# Use of Funds

For a $25,000 grant, funds would be used to turn the current public prototype into a stronger open-source benchmark and toolkit for practical AI agent safety.

## Allocation

- 40% engineering: evaluator, adapters, CI templates, rule packs
- 25% benchmark curation: real-world trace collection, validation, redaction
- 15% documentation: tutorials, guides, and case studies for independent builders
- 10% web3 and infrastructure safety rule packs
- 10% community feedback, maintenance, and release work

## What the grant unlocks

Without funding, this remains a side project. With funding, the work becomes a usable public good: stronger examples, better documentation, broader benchmark coverage, and enough quality for other builders to trust, adopt, and extend.

## The open-source return

Every dollar produces public artifacts: open rules, open traces, open documentation, open benchmarks. No lock-in, no proprietary dependencies, no hidden evaluation systems. The output compounds in the open for every builder who uses it.
